Section 05

Key Features — Deep Dive

🔍
1. Smart Auto-Detection Engine
Automatically identifies sensitive attributes (gender, race, age, caste, religion, nationality, disability, marital status) and decision columns from any CSV by keyword matching column names. Handles 20+ synonym variations. User can override before analysis.
📈
2. Four-Metric Bias Computation (fairlearn + scipy)
For each sensitive attribute: Demographic Parity Difference (DPD) — measures equal positive outcome rates across groups. Equalized Odds Difference (EOD) — measures equal true/false positive rates. Disparate Impact Ratio (DIR) — 80% rule compliance. Cramér's V — statistical association strength (why bias happens).
🤖
3. Gemini 2.5 Flash AI — Plain English Explanations
Three parallel Gemini calls (asyncio.gather): explanation of findings, prioritized fix suggestions (title + description + impact + effort), and executive summary for PDF. 20-key pool with automatic rotation on quota errors. 2-second wait on daily quota exhaustion before retrying next key.
📊
4. Bias Drivers Panel — "Why Bias Happens"
Cramér's V-based ranking shows which attribute contributes most to bias. Animated horizontal bars with color-coded risk. Ranked from highest to lowest influence. Judges can instantly see that "Race" drives 67% of bias vs "Age" at 23% — a key differentiator vs all competing tools.
⚠
5. Most Disadvantaged Group Identification
Per attribute, FairLens identifies which specific group has the lowest positive rate and compares it to the most advantaged group: "Black group gets 31% positive rate vs White group (68%) — a 37% gap." This is actionable intelligence that raw metric numbers don't provide.
🪄
6. What-If Bias Simulator (Interactive)
Click any attribute to simulate removing it from the model. Score updates in real-time with spring animation. Impact message explains: "+18 point improvement by removing Race from decisions — reduces risk from HIGH to MEDIUM." No other free tool offers this interactive simulation.
📄
7. Professional PDF Report Generator (ReportLab)
One-click generates a structured PDF with: 2-column header (score ring + risk badge), compliance table (EU AI Act / EEOC / ISO with PASS/FAIL in green/red), per-attribute metrics table, Gemini AI summary paragraphs, and prioritized remediation recommendations. No emojis (breaks PDF rendering) — pure professional layout.
🔐
8. Regulatory Compliance Auto-Check
Automatically evaluates EU AI Act Art. 10 (DPD < 0.1, DIR > 0.8), EEOC 4/5 Rule (DIR ≥ 0.80), and ISO 42001 (audit documentation). Displays PASS/FAIL badges with exact metric values and legal citations. Links to official regulation documents.
🌌
9. Live Demo — No Login Required
Three pre-loaded real datasets (COMPAS Recidivism, Adult Income UCI, German Credit) run instantly. Demo page shows all features: compliance panel, bias drivers, what-if simulator, fix suggestions, full Gemini explanation. Perfect for judges to evaluate without signup friction.
📱
10. Fully Responsive — Mobile + Dark/Light Mode
Dashboard converts to mobile card layout with hamburger slide-in drawer. All charts scale. Dark mode (default) and light mode with comprehensive CSS variable overrides. Theme toggle in dashboard sidebar. All metric bars, compliance badges, and charts work in both modes.
Section 06

The Science: What Each Metric Means

Demographic Parity Difference (DPD)Threshold: |DPD| < 0.1
Measures the difference in positive outcome rates between the most and least advantaged groups. DPD = 0 means perfect parity. DPD = 0.309 (COMPAS Race) means Black defendants are flagged at a rate 30.9 percentage points higher than White defendants.
Real meaning: How unfairly is the algorithm distributing positive/negative outcomes?
Equalized Odds Difference (EOD)Threshold: |EOD| < 0.1
Measures whether the algorithm is equally accurate for all groups — both when predicting true positives and true negatives. EOD = 0 means the model makes errors equally across groups. High EOD means the model is more wrong for some groups than others.
Real meaning: Is the algorithm equally reliable regardless of which group you belong to?
Disparate Impact Ratio (DIR)Threshold: DIR ≥ 0.80
Ratio of positive rates: min_group_rate / max_group_rate. DIR = 0.552 means the least advantaged group gets positive outcomes at only 55.2% the rate of the most advantaged group — far below the 80% legal threshold.
Real meaning: The US EEOC 4/5 Rule — protected groups must receive positive outcomes at ≥80% the rate of the best group.
Cramér's V — Bias Contribution %Novel: Why bias happens
Chi-squared based statistical association between the sensitive column and the outcome. Higher % means this column is more strongly driving unequal outcomes. Used exclusively in FairLens to rank which attribute is the primary bias driver — something no competing tool provides.
Real meaning: Which column is most responsible for the bias you see?
