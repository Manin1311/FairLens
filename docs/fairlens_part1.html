Section 01

Executive Summary

FairLens is a production-ready web platform that allows any organization — without ML expertise — to upload a dataset, automatically detect algorithmic discrimination, receive Gemini AI explanations in plain English, check regulatory compliance, and download a professional PDF audit report.

Core Idea: Every AI system making decisions about people — loans, hiring, healthcare, criminal justice — can discriminate. FairLens makes it dead simple to find and fix that discrimination before it causes real harm or legal liability.

🔍 Detect

Upload any CSV. Get DPD, EOD, DIR + Cramér's V bias scores across all sensitive attributes in seconds.

🤖 Explain

Google Gemini 2.5 Flash translates complex fairness metrics into plain English any decision-maker can understand.

📈 Fix

Prioritized remediation steps, compliance status, What-If simulator, and downloadable PDF for legal teams.

Section 02

The Problem: AI Discrimination is a Global Crisis

Critical Reality: AI systems make life-altering decisions for billions of people — yet 87% of organizations have no process to test if their AI is fair. This isn't a hypothetical risk. It's happening right now.

Real-World Cases of Proven AI Bias

⚖
COMPAS Criminal Justice Tool
Black defendants were 2× more likely to be falsely flagged as high recidivism risk than white defendants. Used in 1,000s of US courtrooms daily.
💼
Amazon Hiring AI (2018)
Systematically downgraded resumes from women. Amazon scrapped it after years of discriminatory use. Cause: biased historical training data.
🏠
Healthcare Risk Scoring
Black patients assigned lower risk scores, denying them care. Affected 200M+ patients. Algorithm used "cost" as proxy for "need."
💰
Loan & Credit Scoring
Gender and race bias in credit AI leads to discriminatory lending — violating EEOC, Equal Credit Opportunity Act, and EU AI Act.

Why Current Solutions Completely Fail

IBM AI Fairness 360, Google What-If Tool, and Microsoft Fairlearn are powerful — but they are Python libraries and CLI tools. They require a data scientist to use them. They produce raw numbers with zero plain-English explanation. They generate no PDF report, no compliance check, and no remediation plan. They solve the wrong problem. The people who need bias audits — HR directors, legal teams, compliance officers, regulators — cannot use any of these tools.

The Real Gap: There is no production-ready web application that makes AI bias auditing accessible to non-technical stakeholders. FairLens fills this gap completely.
